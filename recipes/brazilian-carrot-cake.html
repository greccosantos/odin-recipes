Brazilian Carrot Cake

Description

This isn’t the carrot cake you’re used to - no raisins, no cream cheese, and no apologies. Brazil’s bolo de cenoura blends carrots into a smooth batter, baked into a moist cake and topped with a slick chocolate glaze. It’s the dessert you didn’t know you needed, pairing better with strong coffee than your average pastry does.

Ingredients

Carrot Cake

  • 10 oz (283 g, about 2 cups chopped) peeled carrots
  • 3 large eggs (about 150 g total)
  • 1 cup (240 g) avocado oil
  • 1¾ cups (350 g) granulated sugar
  • 2 cups (240 g) all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.5 g) table salt
  • 1 tablespoon (12 g) double-acting baking powder

Chocolate Glaze

  • 1 cup (170 g) semi-sweet or dark chocolate chips (50-70% cocoa)
  • 6 tablespoons (85 g) unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons (40 g) light corn syrup

Steps

Carrot Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Lightly grease a 9×13-inch baking pan and line it with parchment paper, ensuring an overhang for easy removal
  2. Place 10 oz peeled and chopped carrots, 3 large eggs, and 1 cup avocado oil in a blender. Blend on high for 1 minute until smooth, ensuring no carrot chunks remain. Add 1¾ cups granulated sugar and blend briefly (about 5 seconds) until incorporated.
  3. Add 2 cups all-purpose flour, ¼ teaspoon table salt, and 1 tablespoon baking powder to the blender. Stir gently with a rubber spatula to partially incorporate, scraping the sides, then blend on low until the batter is smooth and evenly mixed with no lumps.
  4. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 30-40 minutes, checking at 30 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Remove from the oven and let the cake cool in the pan until just warm to the touch, about 1 hour.
  5. Prepare the chocolate glaze while the cake cools, then apply as directed below.

Chocolate Glaze

  1. Combine 1 cup chocolate chips, 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, and 2 tablespoons light corn syrup in a double boiler (a heatproof bowl set over a pot with 1-2 inches of simmering water). Stir constantly until melted and smooth. Alternatively, microwave in a microwave-safe bowl in 20-second intervals, stirring between each, until smooth.
  2. Remove the glaze from heat and let it cool for 5-10 minutes until slightly thickened but still pour-able. Pour half the glaze over the just-warm cake, spreading it evenly with a spatula. Gradually add the remaining glaze, smoothing gently to avoid spillage, as the glaze thickens as it cools.
