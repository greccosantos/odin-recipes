Brigadeiro

Description

Meet Brazil’s ultimate crowd-pleaser: brigadeiros! Made with sweetened condensed milk, cocoa, and a touch of butter, they’re cooked into a rich, creamy fudge, then coated in sprinkles. They’re still one of my favorite desserts and the star of every Brazilian celebration.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz or 397 g) of sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 tablespoons (about 20 g) of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon (about 14 g) of unsalted butter
  • 100 g (3.5 oz) of chocolate sprinkles
  • 20-25 small paper candy cups (size #5, about 1-inch diameter base)

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine 1 can (14 oz or 397 g) of sweetened condensed milk, 3 tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder, and 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter. Cook over low to medium-low heat, stirring constantly with a spatula, for about 15-20 minutes, until the mixture thickens and you can see the bottom of the pan for 2-3 seconds when dragging the spatula through it.
  2. Pour the mixture onto a lightly buttered plate and let it cool at room temperature for about 3 hours, until firm and cool to the touch.
  3. Lightly butter your hands, then shape the cooled mixture into 1-inch balls (about 1-2 teaspoons each). Roll each ball in 100 g of chocolate sprinkles spread on a plate to coat evenly.
  4. Place the sprinkle-coated balls in small paper candy cups (size #5) for serving.
